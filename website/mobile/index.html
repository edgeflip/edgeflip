what we do

edgeflip helps non-profits, charities, and other good causes use the social networks of their existing supporters to increase fundraising, more effectively recruit and mobilize volunteers, and expand their reach & supporter base.

We initially developed this technology to help the Obama Campaign maximize the effectivness of its social media presence by asking supporters to reach out to their friends with personally-targeted content about registering to vote, the President's agenda, or how and where to vote. Because of the reach and power of these friend-to-friend contacts, the campaign's digital director described Targeted Sharing to TIME magazine as "the most groundbreaking piece of technology developed for this campaign."

who we are

Rayid Ghani: As Chief Scientist for Obama for America, Rayid conceived of targeted sharing and guided its development and implementation. Before OFA, he was the Chief Scientist at Accenture.

Matthew Rattigan: The Obama Campaign's lead analyst on the targeted sharing project. Before OFA, Matthew earned his PhD in Computer Science from University of Massachusetts at Amherst.

Kit Rodolfa: The digital modeling & engineering director for the Obama Campaign. Before OFA, Kit earned an MPP from Harvard's Kennedy School and a PhD in Stem Cell Biology from Harvard.
