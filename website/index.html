what we do

edgeflip helps non-profits, charities, and other good causes use the social networks of their existing supporters to increase fundraising, more effectively recruit and mobilize volunteers, and expand their reach & supporter base.

We initially developed this technology to help the Obama Campaign maximize the effectivness of its social media presence by asking supporters to reach out to their friends with personally-targeted content about registering to vote, the President's agenda, or how and where to vote. Because of the reach and power of these friend-to-friend contacts, the campaign's digital director described Targeted Sharing to TIME magazine as "the most groundbreaking piece of technology developed for this campaign."

who we are

Rayid Ghani: As Chief Scientist for Obama for America, Rayid conceived of targeted sharing and guided its development and implementation. Before OFA, he was the Chief Scientist at Accenture.

Matthew Rattigan: The Obama Campaign's lead analyst on the targeted sharing project. Before OFA, Matthew earned his PhD in Computer Science from University of Massachusetts at Amherst.

Kit Rodolfa: The digital modeling & engineering director for the Obama Campaign. Before OFA, Kit earned an MPP from Harvard's Kennedy School and a PhD in Stem Cell Biology from Harvard.

>

Rayid Ghani

Rayid served as the Chief Scientist for President Obama's re-election campaign begining in July 2011. Among his numerous contributions to the campaign's efforts was the conception of a new way to use the social networks of the President's supporters to greatly expand the campaign's reach with highly targeted campaign materials. During his year and a half on the campaign, Rayid guided the development, testing, and implementation of this Targeted Sharing Technology. Prior to joining the campaign, Rayid was a Senior Research Scientist and Director of Analytics research at Accenture Labs where he led a technology research team focused on applied R&D in analytics, machine learning, and data mining for large-scale & emerging business problems in various industries including healthcare, retail & CPG, manufacturing, intelligence, and financial services.

Matthew Rattigan

Matthew joined the Obama Campaign in December 2011, where he worked as the lead Analyst on the Targeted Sharing project. Matt single-handedly built the initial prototype of the tool and oversaw the optimization and modeling for the final product. Before the campaign, Matthew earned his PhD in Computer Science from the University of Massachusetts at Amherst, where his work in the laboratory of Prof. David Jensen focused on causal inference in social graph and network data. Matthew's areas of expertise include machine learning, knowledge discovery and data mining, causal inference, social network analysis, statistical relational learning, experimental design.

Kit Rodolfa

Kit joined the Obama Campaign in June 2011 and served as the Digital Modeling and Engineering Director, a that role included acting as the Analytics Department's lead for coordinating the campaign's online and offline efforts. Before the campaign, he earned a Master's in Public Policy from Harvard's Kennedy School of Government, where his thesis focused on helping a national healthcare non-profit navigate the complexities of Medicaid reimbursement and the Affordable Care Act. Kit's background is in the natural sciences and he holds a PhD in Stem Cell Biology from Harvard and an MPhil in Chemistry from Cambridge. His doctoral work earned recognition as the journal Science's 2008 "Breathrough of the Year" and was featured on the cover of TIME magazine.
